Energy Intelligence Node (EIN) is an open-source AI pipeline that checks renewable-energy technology claims against fundamental physical laws — flagging the exaggerated, the contradictory, and the physically impossible, in seconds.

View on GitHub How it works

The problem

The energy transition is slowed by two things: physical engineering bottlenecks, and corporate greenwashing. Investors, banks and governments fund projects based on technical claims that are often inflated or physically impossible — wasting capital that the planet cannot afford to lose.

Exaggerated metrics

"99% efficiency", "1000 Wh/kg" — claims that beat the laws of physics flood pitch decks and press releases.

Expensive verification

Rigorous due diligence needs in-house scientists or costly consultants — out of reach for smaller funds and developing nations.

Real consequences

New regulation (EU Green Claims Directive) makes unsubstantiated claims a legal liability. The cost of being fooled is rising.

How it works

EIN ingests papers (ArXiv), patents (USPTO) and technical press, extracts the quantitative claims, and runs a deterministic Contradiction Engine that checks each metric against a knowledge base of physical limits. Every verdict is transparent and cites the exact law involved.

CLAIMED

No violation detected. The claim is within known physical limits (not a guarantee of truth — a green light to investigate further).

DISPUTED

Exceeds a known practical ceiling (e.g. demonstrated battery energy density). Requires documentary verification.

DEBUNKED

Violates a fundamental physical law — physically impossible. A hard red flag for greenwashing.

Grounded in physics, not opinion

The engine is 100% deterministic and runs locally on a standard laptop — no expensive proprietary APIs. Each rule is auditable and easy to extend.

What you get

Credibility score (0–100)

Every claim gets an explainable score based on the physical verdict, source type, and whether it reports verifiable metrics.

Due-diligence reports

Auto-generated reports a non-expert can read in seconds — with a clear recommendation and red-flag warnings.

Queryable REST API

Endpoints for /claims, /contradictions, /score and /report (FastAPI).

Open source (MIT)

Fully transparent and auditable. Backed by 46 automated tests. Extend it to new technologies and data sources.

Why this matters: energy justice

Governments and communities in the Global South often lack the technical capacity to audit whether a multi-million-dollar green technology actually works — or if it is just venture-capital marketing. EIN levels the playing field with institutional-grade technology intelligence that anyone can run for free. Our long-term mission: democratize access to powerful AI across the Global South, starting from Riobamba, Ecuador.
